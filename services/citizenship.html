American citizenship

There are many benefits to becoming a U.S. citizen. Unfortunately, the process of gaining citizenship can be time consuming and requires multiple steps. Many immigrants work for months or even years on achieving the citizenship they long for.

If you want to become a U.S. citizen, contact Attorney Eric Price. Our skilled immigration attorneys have the knowledge and resources you need to move forward with your citizenship goals.

The Process of U.S. Citizenship

Before you can become a U.S. citizen, you must apply for citizenship or naturalization. In order to apply for citizenship, you must meet the following requirements:

  • * Have lived in the U.S. continuosly for at least five years
  • * Have lived in the same state for at least three months
  • * Have a basic understanding of English reading and writing
  • * Have a basic understanding of U.S. history, civics, and laws
  • * Be of good moral character

Depending on your situation, you may apply for citizenship via naturalization or a Certificate of Citizenship. The best way to determine which option is right for you is to discuss your case with a qualified immigration attorney.

Each process has different requirements, and it is important that you understand the process and your responsibilities. An attorney can walk you through the process and help avoid obstacles.

Do I Need an Attorney for U.S. Citizenship?

Many people ask if it is necessary to hire an attorney for U.S. citizenship. There is no legal requirement that you hire an attorney. However, like most legal processes, it is a good idea to have an attorney in your corner in case you have questions or run into problems.

Many people who are seeking citizenship overlook important red flags, such as tax issues, previous crimes or offenses on their record, or misunderstanding the documents.

Working with Attorney Eric Price can help avoid red flags and ensure that you meet your citizenship goals.

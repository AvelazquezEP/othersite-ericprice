A LAW OF FREEDOM OF INFORMATION

The Freedom of information ACT, or FOIA, is often used by journalists, historians, and the legal industry to uncover information that may not otherwise be available. And while that is useful, it is not the extent of the FOIA's uses.

The FOIA is algo a useful tool for immigration attorneys, civil litigation attorneys, and individuals involved in the legal industry. More specifically, the FOIA is a useful tool to obtain immigration records. Your immigration file is called an "A-file".

At Attorney Eric Proce, we lnow just how murky the waters of government record-keeping can be. Our attorneys know how to file and FOIA request and obtain important information that is relevant to your immigration case.

FOIA and immigration

It can be incredibly frustrating to find out that the government has background information you didn't even know exist. Unfortunately, sometimes something in your record you didn't even know about can hinder your immigration or citizenship plans.

And then there are times when you need extensive background information for the purposes of immigration One example is if you are seeking asylum. In these cases, obtaining your immigration file is imperative.

If you are having trouble moving through the immigration process, it can be a good idea to request a copy of your immigration file. Making an FOIA request involves the following:

  • * Determine what information you really need
  • * Determine which government agency is most likely to have that information
  • * Identify the reasons why you need the immigration file and/or background information

The next step is making your FOIA request. The formis G-639, but making a request online is much safer and more efficient. You should know that you may not have access to all of your information. Some information may only be available through certain agencies like customs and Border Protection
